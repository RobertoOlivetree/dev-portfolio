## Overview

**GEOInsightLab** is an open research platform developed to integrate
Geographic Information Science, Spatial Data Science, machine learning,
Explainable Artificial Intelligence and interactive geographic visualisation.

The platform was created within the doctoral research project
*Data Science in Spatial Literacy* and provides a reproducible framework
for analysing, interpreting and communicating geographic information.

## Research Purpose

GEOInsightLab explores how spatial data science can strengthen spatial
literacy and improve the interpretation of complex geographic phenomena.

The platform combines quantitative analysis, spatial reasoning,
interactive visualisation and explainable artificial intelligence to
support research, education and evidence-based territorial decision-making.

## Main Components

- Exploratory spatial data analysis
- Building-level urban analysis
- Pedestrian accessibility assessment
- Spatial clustering
- Supervised machine learning
- Urban attractiveness indicators
- Explainable AI with LIME and SHAP
- Interactive geographic visualisation
- Reproducible computational workflows

## Case Study

The initial case study focuses on the municipality of Porto, Portugal.

The analysis integrates:

- building-level spatial data;
- population aged 65 and over;
- urban services;
- pedestrian accessibility;
- spatial statistics;
- machine-learning models;
- explainable AI methods.

## Interactive Application

The online application provides access to selected results through
interactive maps, indicators and visual explanations.

The complete computational workflow is not executed online because the
original spatial datasets, pedestrian network and intermediate outputs
require substantial processing resources.

The application therefore uses selected and pre-processed results,
allowing the project to remain accessible without depending on a local
computer.

## Open Science

The project follows Open Science and reproducible research principles.

Whenever licensing and publication conditions permit, the following are
made available:

- source code;
- computational notebooks;
- methodological documentation;
- selected datasets;
- interactive demonstrations.
